The first to develop the base of the Arabic language (Abu Al-Aswad Al-Du’ali) is considered the first scholar in the Arabic language, who was interested in establishing rules for it, based on a hadith that took place between him and Imam Ali bin Abi Talib - may God be pleased with him -, and Imam Ali said to him: Divide the speech into a letter, a noun, and a verb, and he continued on This grammar, and from here came the first idea of ​​the science of grammar, to become one of the basic sciences on which the Arabic grammar is based in its correct form.

How old is the Arabic language? The classical Arabic language dates back to about 1,600 years, while its origins go back much earlier, and it is the most spoken and spoken language within the group of Semitic languages ​​and one of the most widespread languages ​​in the world and is spoken by more than 467 million people distributed over the Arab world and many other neighboring regions. such as Turkey, Chad, Mali, Senegal, Eritrea and Ethiopia
